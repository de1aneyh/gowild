How this works

Frontier only lets you check one route and one date at a time, and it has no public availability feed that a webpage can read directly. This launcher solves the tedious part: it lists your airport's whole Frontier network for whatever date you pick, lets you filter to nonstop, and gives every destination a one‑click link that opens Frontier's real search with your route and date already filled in — so you can eyeball the whole map fast instead of typing 40 searches.

Reading "GoWild now" and the fares

GoWild has two ways to book. Day‑of: seats open the day before departure (they drop around 12:00 AM local the night before) at the classic ~$0.01 + tax fare. Early booking: Frontier also releases select seats further ahead for a per‑segment fee, so a future date can absolutely show GoWild — just at a higher fare (e.g. ~$59 domestic / ~$74 to San Juan on the Jul 25 example). Each green chip shows that flight's GoWild fare, so you can tell a near‑free day‑of seat from a paid early‑booking one at a glance. The column reflects the last scan () — pick any date and scan it for current fares; the Frontier link always confirms live.

The Departs (nonstop) column lists each nonstop route's departure times and flight length from the last scan (e.g. 1:22p1h45m). When a GoWild seat is open on a flight, its time chip turns green — so you can see at a glance not just where you can go, but when. Connecting destinations show "via connection"; open them on Frontier for itinerary times.

Blackout dates come first

Even on a wide‑open route, GoWild simply doesn't work on blackout dates — they sit on every major holiday and peak weekend. This tool checks your chosen date against Frontier's published 2026–27 blackout calendar and warns you before you waste time. Today's zero results are a blackout, not a bug: July 2–6 is blacked out; the next GoWild‑eligible day is July 7.
